# MASA-DOC-035: Crew Profile: Medical Officer Lucía Mendoza

## Personal Background and Nationality

Medical Officer Lucía Mendoza, a 37-year-old Chilean national, serves as the primary medical authority aboard Kuntur Station. Her Chilean nationality reflects MASA's commitment to regional representation, with Mendoza bringing a decade of specialized experience to the South American space program. As the sole medical officer, she operates from the Tucan module, which houses the station's medical bay and life sciences research facilities. Her presence ensures continuous medical oversight for the six-person crew, with her expertise extending beyond routine health monitoring to emergency surgical intervention—a critical capability given the station's high altitude and the inherent risks of long-duration spaceflight.

## Specialization and Experience

Mendoza's specialization in Space Medicine and Emergency Surgery equips her to address the unique physiological challenges of microgravity environments. Her 10 years of experience include extensive training in trauma response, with particular emphasis on procedures adapted for confined spaces and limited resources. This expertise is vital aboard Kuntur Station, where evacuation to terrestrial medical facilities is not feasible. Her role intersects with multiple operational protocols, particularly MASA-SEC-005 (Medical Emergency Response), which she may activate at high severity when crew health is compromised. Her surgical capabilities are complemented by her proficiency in telemedicine, allowing her to consult with ground-based specialists during complex procedures.

## Mission History

Mendoza's operational history includes two prior missions: Condor-4 and Condor-6, both of which were foundational in establishing MASA's human spaceflight capabilities. During Condor-4, she served as the medical officer for a long-duration mission, where she developed protocols for in-flight medical diagnostics that are now standard across MASA operations. Her participation in Condor-6 further refined her expertise in managing crew health during extended missions, with a focus on mitigating the effects of radiation exposure and muscle atrophy. These missions provided her with firsthand experience in executing MASA-OPS-012 (Crew Medical Examination), a procedure she now oversees aboard Kuntur Station with enhanced precision due to the station's expanded medical facilities.

## Current Assignment and Responsibilities

As the medical officer assigned to the Tucan module, Mendoza's responsibilities encompass both routine and emergency medical operations. She conducts daily health assessments as part of MASA-OPS-012, monitoring vital signs and physiological adaptations to microgravity. Her role extends to overseeing the station's life support systems, particularly the oxygen and CO2 scrubbing systems, which directly impact crew health. In the event of a medical emergency, she is authorized to initiate MASA-SEC-005, which includes coordinating with the station's engineer, Pavel Kozlov, to ensure life support stability during treatment. Additionally, she collaborates with Specialist Fátima Al-Hassan in the Quetzal module to manage biological experiments, ensuring that research activities do not compromise crew safety.

Mendoza's responsibilities also include maintaining the station's medical inventory, a task that intersects with MASA-OPS-013 (Supply Request and Inventory Management). She ensures that critical medical supplies, such as emergency trauma kits and pharmaceuticals, are replenished during resupply missions. Her role is further integrated with station-wide protocols, such as MASA-SEC-019 (Biological Contamination Containment), which she may activate if a hazardous biological agent is detected in the Tucan module. This protocol requires her to coordinate with Commander Santiago Reyes in the Condor module to implement containment measures, demonstrating the interconnected nature of her medical oversight with broader station operations.

In summary, Lucía Mendoza's role as Medical Officer is indispensable to the operational integrity of Kuntur Station. Her specialized training, combined with her experience from Condor-4 and Condor-6, ensures that the crew remains medically supported in an environment where terrestrial medical intervention is not an option. Her responsibilities span routine health monitoring, emergency response, and collaboration with other crew members to maintain the station's life support and research capabilities.
