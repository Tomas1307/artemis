# MASA-DOC-019: Maintenance and Calibration Procedures

## MASA-OPS-002: Scheduled Preventive Maintenance

The backbone of Kuntur Station's operational reliability is MASA-OPS-002 (Scheduled Preventive Maintenance), a comprehensive weekly procedure conducted every Sunday with a duration of 240 minutes. This protocol ensures the continuous functionality of life support, power, and thermal systems across all modules—Condor, Quetzal, Jaguar, Colibri, and Tucan. The responsibility for executing this procedure falls to Systems Engineer Pavel Kozlov, whose expertise in mechanical and electrical systems is critical for identifying potential failures before they escalate into emergencies.

The 240-minute duration is carefully calibrated to allow thorough inspection without disrupting the station's daily operations. Life support systems are checked for filter integrity, airflow consistency, and pressure regulation, while power cells undergo voltage and capacity testing to prevent unexpected failures that could trigger MASA-SEC-006 (Power Grid Critical Failure). Thermal systems are evaluated for coolant levels, heat exchanger efficiency, and radiator performance, as thermal mismanagement can lead to conditions requiring MASA-SEC-003 (Fire and Thermal Runaway Suppression).

The weekly frequency is based on MASA's analysis of system degradation rates in microgravity environments. Without this regular intervention, minor inefficiencies in life support systems could compound, potentially leading to oxygen depletion scenarios that would necessitate MASA-SEC-002 (Oxygen Depletion Response). The scope of this procedure spans all modules, ensuring that even secondary systems in less frequently used areas are maintained to operational standards.

## MASA-OPS-010: Life Support Maintenance

While MASA-OPS-002 provides broad coverage, MASA-OPS-010 (Life Support Maintenance) offers a deeper, more specialized focus on the Jaguar module's life support infrastructure. Conducted bi-weekly with a duration of 180 minutes, this procedure targets the O2 generation systems, CO2 scrubbers, and water recycling equipment that are critical for crew survival. The Jaguar module, being the primary habitat for Systems Engineer Pavel Kozlov, requires this enhanced maintenance schedule due to its continuous occupancy and the sensitivity of its life support components.

The 180-minute duration allows for comprehensive testing of oxygen generation efficiency, CO2 scrubber absorption rates, and water recycling purity levels. Any deviation in these systems could rapidly escalate to conditions requiring MASA-SEC-014 (CO2 Scrubber Failure Protocol) or MASA-SEC-015 (Water Recycling System Failure). The bi-weekly frequency was established based on the observed degradation rates of chemical scrubbers and membrane filters in microgravity, where particulate accumulation occurs more rapidly than in Earth-based systems.

During this procedure, Engineer Kozlov performs calibration checks on oxygen sensors to ensure they maintain accuracy within the required range for crew safety. CO2 levels are verified to remain below the established threshold, as higher concentrations can impair crew cognition and trigger MASA-SEC-009 (Toxic Atmosphere Detection). Water recycling systems are tested for microbial contamination, with any anomalies potentially leading to MASA-SEC-019 (Biological Contamination Containment).

## MASA-OPS-014: Software Update and System Calibration

The digital infrastructure of Kuntur Station requires equally rigorous maintenance through MASA-OPS-014 (Software Update and System Calibration). This monthly procedure, lasting 240 minutes, is conducted in the Condor and Colibri modules, which house the station's primary command and control systems. Responsibility for this operation is shared between Systems Engineer Pavel Kozlov and Pilot Ana Valdivia, reflecting the critical nature of both software integrity and system calibration for station operations.

The 240-minute duration accounts for the time required to safely update flight control software, communication protocols, and environmental monitoring algorithms. During this period, the station operates in a reduced operational mode to prevent software conflicts that could trigger MASA-SEC-016 (Navigation Deviation Alert) or MASA-SEC-007 (Communication Blackout Protocol). The monthly frequency aligns with MASA's software development cycle, ensuring that critical security patches and performance optimizations are implemented without overwhelming the crew with constant updates.

System calibration during this procedure includes verifying the accuracy of attitude control sensors, which must maintain precision to prevent orbital drift. Communication arrays are tested for signal strength and bandwidth efficiency, as any degradation could compromise the station's ability to relay critical data during emergencies. Power management systems are recalibrated to ensure load balancing remains within the expected capacity range, preventing conditions that might lead to MASA-SEC-018 (Power Fluctuation Response).

## Maintenance Tool Inventory

The execution of all maintenance procedures relies on a comprehensive inventory of specialized tools maintained in each module. The Condor module houses the primary tool locker, containing torque wrenches calibrated for microgravity operations, multimeter sets for electrical diagnostics, and precision pressure gauges for life support system testing. The Jaguar module maintains a secondary inventory focused on life support maintenance, including gas analyzers for atmospheric composition testing and microbial sampling kits for water system monitoring.

Each tool undergoes weekly verification as part of MASA-OPS-002 to ensure calibration remains within manufacturer specifications. Torque wrenches, for instance, must maintain accuracy to prevent over-tightening of critical fasteners that could lead to structural stress requiring MASA-SEC-008 (Structural Integrity Alert). Electrical test equipment is verified against known standards to ensure voltage measurements remain accurate, as incorrect readings could mask developing power system failures.

The inventory system employs RFID tracking to monitor tool locations and usage patterns. This data is integrated into the station's maintenance management system, allowing Engineer Kozlov to anticipate tool replacement needs before they become critical. The system also logs tool usage during maintenance procedures, providing valuable data for post-maintenance reviews and continuous improvement of operational protocols.

## Reduced Operation Mode During Updates

The implementation of MASA-OPS-014 necessitates placing the station in a reduced operational mode to ensure system stability during software updates and calibration. This mode involves temporarily disabling non-critical systems and transferring essential functions to backup units. The Condor module's command systems remain operational, with redundant processors handling navigation and environmental control. Communication systems in Colibri operate in a degraded mode, maintaining only essential voice and telemetry links with ground stations.

During this period, crew activities are restricted to essential operations only. Scientific experiments in the Quetzal module are paused, and cargo operations are suspended. The Tucan module's medical systems remain fully operational, as medical emergencies cannot be deferred. This reduced mode is carefully designed to maintain crew safety while allowing the necessary system updates to proceed without interruption.

The transition to reduced operation mode begins before the scheduled update window, allowing sufficient time for system stabilization. Engineer Kozlov and Pilot Valdivia conduct a final systems check to verify that all critical functions have successfully transferred to backup systems. Throughout the update process, real-time telemetry is monitored for any anomalies that might indicate software conflicts or calibration errors. Upon completion, a comprehensive system restart is performed, with each module's systems brought back online in a controlled sequence to prevent power surges that could trigger MASA-SEC-018 (Power Fluctuation Response).

The return to full operational status is confirmed through a series of functional tests, including verification of navigation accuracy, communication link quality, and life support system performance. Only after all systems have been verified to operate within their normal parameters is the station declared fully operational. This careful approach to system updates and calibration ensures that Kuntur Station maintains its operational integrity while incorporating the latest software improvements and system optimizations.
