# MASA-DOC-021: Docking, Cargo, and Supply Procedures

## MASA-OPS-003: Docking and Undocking Procedure

The MASA-OPS-003 (Docking and Undocking Procedure) governs all operations related to the arrival and departure of supply vessels at the Vicuna docking port, ensuring structural integrity and communication continuity throughout the process. This procedure is executed as needed, with an average duration of 180 minutes from initial approach to secure docking or undocking completion. Primary responsibility rests with Pilot Ana Valdivia, who oversees the docking sequence from the Colibri module, while Commander Santiago Reyes provides oversight from the Condor command center. The Vicuna module serves as the primary docking interface, with Colibri providing navigational support and communication relay capabilities.

The procedure begins with a comprehensive pre-docking checklist that verifies structural integrity of both the Vicuna docking port and the approaching vessel. Structural checks include confirmation of docking collar alignment, seal integrity, and pressure equalization readiness. Communication systems are verified to ensure uninterrupted data and voice links between the supply vessel, Vicuna, Colibri, and MASA ground control. Any anomalies in structural or communication systems trigger MASA-SEC-008 (Structural Integrity Alert) or MASA-SEC-007 (Communication Blackout Protocol) respectively, depending on severity.

During the docking approach sequence, the supply vessel maintains a low relative velocity during the final approach phase, with Pilot Valdivia monitoring telemetry from Colibri's navigation systems. The Vicuna docking port features redundant pressure sensors that continuously monitor the docking interface. Any pressure deviation during docking triggers MASA-SEC-013 (Docking Bay Pressure Anomaly), requiring immediate abort procedures. The docking sequence concludes with a series of structural integrity tests, including a pressure hold test to verify seal integrity before crew access is permitted.

Undocking procedures follow a similar structural and communication verification process, with additional emphasis on propulsion system checks to ensure clean separation. The procedure requires a communication window with MASA ground control before undocking initiation, as specified in MASA-OPS-008 (Communication Window Management). Throughout both docking and undocking operations, the Colibri module maintains continuous telemetry monitoring, with Engineer Pavel Kozlov standing by in Jaguar to address any life support or power system anomalies that might arise from the docking operations.

## MASA-OPS-005: Cargo Transfer and Inventory Update

Following successful docking, MASA-OPS-005 (Cargo Transfer and Inventory Update) commences under the responsibility of Specialist Kai Nakamura and Specialist Fátima Al-Hassan. This procedure, which typically requires 180 minutes to complete, involves the systematic transfer of supplies from the docked vessel to Vicuna's storage facilities, followed by comprehensive inventory reconciliation. The procedure's scope is limited to the Vicuna module, though it requires structural integrity verification at multiple stages to ensure safe cargo handling.

Cargo transfer begins with a preliminary manifest verification against the expected delivery, cross-referenced with the inventory management system in Colibri. Each cargo container undergoes visual inspection for structural damage before transfer, with any compromised containers isolated for further evaluation. The Vicuna module's storage capacity is organized into standardized containers, each with a weight limit to facilitate manual handling in microgravity conditions. Specialists Nakamura and Al-Hassan work in tandem, with one managing the physical transfer while the other updates the digital inventory in real-time.

The inventory update process involves scanning each item's RFID tag and verifying its condition against the cargo manifest. Discrepancies between the manifest and actual received items trigger an immediate reconciliation process, with Commander Reyes notified of any significant variances. The procedure concludes with an updated inventory report transmitted to MASA ground control and integrated into the station's resource management system. This updated inventory directly feeds into MASA-OPS-013 (Supply Request and Inventory Management), ensuring accurate supply level tracking for future resupply missions.

Structural integrity checks are performed during cargo transfer, with particular attention to the docking port's load-bearing capacity. The Vicuna module's storage racks are designed to distribute weight evenly, with maximum load limits clearly marked. Any structural concerns during cargo transfer immediately halt operations and trigger MASA-SEC-008 (Structural Integrity Alert), requiring engineering evaluation before resuming transfer activities.

## MASA-OPS-013: Supply Request and Inventory Management

MASA-OPS-013 (Supply Request and Inventory Management) represents the proactive component of Kuntur Station's logistics chain, ensuring continuous operational readiness through monthly inventory audits and timely supply requests. This procedure, conducted by Commander Reyes and Specialist Al-Hassan, has a duration of 120 minutes and encompasses both the Vicuna storage facilities and Colibri's communication systems for data transmission. The procedure is triggered either on a monthly schedule or when any critical supply category falls below 30% of its designated reserve level.

The inventory management process begins with a comprehensive physical count of all consumables, including food supplies, medical provisions, and experimental materials. Each item is cross-referenced against the digital inventory system, with particular attention to expiration dates and usage rates. Food supplies are categorized by nutritional content and shelf life, with a reserve requirement for all essential nutrients. Medical supplies maintain a reserve for critical medications and a reserve for general medical consumables.

When inventory levels for any critical category fall below the 30% threshold, an automated alert is generated in the station's resource management system. Commander Reyes reviews these alerts and compiles a prioritized supply request, which is then transmitted to MASA ground control through Colibri's communication array. The supply request includes detailed consumption data, projected usage rates, and justification for each requested item. Ground control typically responds within 48 hours with a confirmed manifest for the next supply mission.

The procedure concludes with an updated inventory baseline and consumption projections, which are integrated into the station's operational planning system. This data directly informs future supply missions and helps optimize storage utilization across the station. The inventory management system also interfaces with MASA-OPS-005 (Cargo Transfer and Inventory Update), ensuring that received supplies are properly accounted for in the station's resource tracking.

## Docking Approach Sequence

The docking approach sequence represents the most critical phase of MASA-OPS-003, requiring precise coordination between the supply vessel, Vicuna docking port, and Colibri navigation systems. The sequence begins when the supply vessel enters the approach corridor, at which point Pilot Valdivia assumes manual control from Colibri's navigation station. The vessel maintains a relative velocity during the initial approach phase, gradually reducing to a low velocity during the final approach phase.

As the vessel approaches the docking corridor, the Vicuna docking port extends its capture ring and activates the alignment guidance system. This system uses laser ranging to provide precise positioning data to both the vessel and Colibri's navigation computers. During the final approach phase, the vessel's relative velocity must not exceed a low threshold, with any deviation triggering an automatic abort sequence. The final approach phase involves a series of micro-adjustments to align the docking collars, with the capture ring engaging at the final contact point.

Upon physical contact, the docking sequence enters the structural verification phase, where the capture ring's latching mechanisms engage in a specific sequence to ensure even load distribution. Pressure equalization begins immediately, with the Vicuna module's systems monitoring the docking interface for any pressure anomalies. The sequence concludes with a structural integrity test, where the docking port is pressurized and held while sensors monitor for any pressure decay.

## Cargo Manifest Verification

Cargo manifest verification represents a critical quality control checkpoint in MASA-OPS-005, ensuring that all supplies received match the expected delivery and meet the station's operational requirements. The verification process begins with a digital comparison between the received manifest and the expected manifest stored in Colibri's inventory system. Any discrepancies in quantity, item description, or packaging configuration are immediately flagged for resolution.

Physical verification involves opening containers to confirm contents match the manifest. Special attention is given to critical supplies, including medical provisions and experimental materials, which undergo verification. Each verified container is scanned using the station's RFID system, updating the digital inventory in real-time. The verification process also includes checking expiration dates against the station's consumption projections, with any items approaching expiration prioritized for immediate use.

The final verification step involves cross-referencing the received supplies against the station's current inventory levels and consumption rates. This analysis helps identify any potential overstock situations that might strain Vicuna's storage capacity or understock conditions that might require expedited resupply. The verified manifest is then transmitted to MASA ground control for confirmation, with any discrepancies resolved through the station's communication protocols before the supply vessel departs.

Throughout the verification process, structural integrity of the Vicuna module is continuously monitored, with particular attention to weight distribution in the storage areas. The module's storage racks are designed to accommodate the expected cargo load while maintaining structural safety margins. Any concerns about weight distribution or structural stress trigger immediate corrective action, potentially including temporary storage in alternative locations until proper distribution can be achieved.
