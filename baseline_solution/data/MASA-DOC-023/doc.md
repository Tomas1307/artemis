# MASA-DOC-023: Mission Report: Cóndor-1 — Initial Orbital Insertion

## Mission Summary

The Cóndor-1 mission, designated as the Initial Orbital Insertion phase of Kuntur Station, commenced on 2029-03-15 and concluded successfully on 2029-03-22, spanning a total of 7 days. This fully automated mission achieved its primary objective of deploying Kuntur Station into the target Low Earth Orbit (LEO) at an altitude of 408 km. The mission's success was marked by the complete pressurization and functional activation of all six modules—Cóndor, Quetzal, Jaguar, Colibrí, Vicuña, and Tucán—without requiring manual intervention from the ground crew or the subsequent human crew assigned to the station.

The automated sequence began with the separation of the Kuntur Station payload from the launch vehicle, followed by a series of pre-programmed orbital adjustment maneuvers to stabilize the station at the designated altitude. The deployment of solar arrays and the initialization of the power distribution grid were critical milestones, ensuring that the station could sustain its operations independently. The mission's success set the stage for the subsequent crewed missions, which would rely on the operational integrity of the systems activated during Cóndor-1.

## Objectives and Outcomes

The primary objective of Cóndor-1 was to deploy Kuntur Station into a stable LEO at 408 km altitude, a target chosen to balance atmospheric drag, orbital decay rates, and accessibility for future resupply missions. The 408 km altitude was selected based on extensive pre-mission modeling, which indicated that this orbit would minimize the frequency of reboost maneuvers while maintaining optimal conditions for scientific observations and communication with ground stations. The mission's fully automated nature was a deliberate design choice to reduce the risk of human error during the critical deployment phase, ensuring that all systems were brought online in a controlled and predictable manner.

The outcome of Cóndor-1 was a complete success, with all six modules achieving full pressurization and functional status post-deployment. The Cóndor module, serving as the central command and control hub, was the first to be activated, followed by the Quetzal and Jaguar modules, which house critical life support and power distribution systems. The Colibrí, Vicuña, and Tucán modules, which support scientific research, cargo storage, and medical operations, respectively, were subsequently brought online. The successful pressurization of all modules was a critical achievement, as it confirmed the structural integrity of the station and the functionality of the environmental control systems, which are essential for supporting human life during subsequent crewed missions.

The absence of any anomalies during the deployment phase allowed the ground team to proceed with the activation of secondary systems, including the communication arrays and the initial calibration of the station's navigation sensors. The success of Cóndor-1 also validated the pre-mission simulations and the redundancy measures built into the station's systems, providing confidence in the station's ability to withstand the rigors of long-duration space operations.
