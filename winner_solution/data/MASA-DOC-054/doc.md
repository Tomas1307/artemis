# MASA-DOC-054: MASA Public Affairs and Educational Outreach

## Public Communication Strategy

Since its inception in 2028, the Mision Aeroespacial Sudamericana Avanzada (MASA) has prioritized transparent and engaging communication with the public. Headquartered in Bogotá, Colombia, MASA recognizes that its mission extends beyond scientific exploration—it is a beacon of inspiration and unity for Latin America and the world. The agency’s public communication strategy is built on three core principles: accessibility, authenticity, and inspiration.

MASA leverages a multi-platform approach to ensure its message reaches diverse audiences. Social media channels, official websites, and partnerships with regional broadcasters allow the agency to share updates, behind-the-scenes content, and educational materials in multiple languages, including Spanish, Portuguese, and English. The agency’s tone is intentionally inclusive, reflecting the cultural diversity of its crew and the nations it represents. By highlighting the human stories behind the science—such as the daily lives of astronauts aboard Kuntur Station—MASA fosters a personal connection between the public and its mission.

## Educational Programs for Latin American Schools

Education is a cornerstone of MASA’s outreach efforts. The agency has developed a suite of programs designed to ignite curiosity in STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) fields among young students across Latin America. These initiatives are tailored to different age groups and educational levels, ensuring that every student can find a point of entry into the world of space exploration.

One of the most popular programs is *“MASA en el Aula”* (MASA in the Classroom), which provides teachers with free, standards-aligned lesson plans that incorporate real-world examples from Kuntur Station. Topics range from the physics of microgravity to the biology of plant growth in space, all presented in a way that is both educational and engaging. Additionally, MASA hosts annual regional competitions, such as the *“Desafío Kuntur”* (Kuntur Challenge), where student teams propose experiments that could be conducted aboard the station. Winning projects are often adapted for actual implementation, giving students a tangible connection to space science.

## Live Stream Events from Kuntur Station

Kuntur Station, orbiting at an altitude of 408 kilometers, serves as a dynamic platform for public engagement. Live stream events from the station are a highlight of MASA’s outreach, offering viewers a rare glimpse into life in low Earth orbit. These broadcasts are carefully planned to showcase not only the scientific work being conducted but also the human experience of living in space.

Events such as *“Conversaciones desde el Cosmos”* (Conversations from the Cosmos) feature crew members from modules like Cóndor, Quetzal, and Jaguar discussing their research, daily routines, and the challenges of spaceflight. These sessions often include interactive Q&A segments, where students and the general public can submit questions in real time. The crew’s diverse backgrounds—representing nations from across Latin America and beyond—add a rich cultural dimension to these exchanges, reinforcing MASA’s role as a unifying force in the region.

## Media Relations and Press Protocols

MASA maintains a proactive and collaborative relationship with the media to ensure accurate and timely reporting on its activities. The agency’s Media Relations Office, based in Bogotá, serves as the primary point of contact for journalists, providing press releases, high-resolution imagery, and expert commentary on mission developments.

Press protocols are designed to balance transparency with operational security. Journalists are granted access to controlled briefings and interviews with mission specialists, though certain sensitive details—such as crew schedules or station logistics—are omitted to respect privacy and safety. MASA also organizes periodic press tours of its ground facilities, offering media representatives an immersive look at the technology and teamwork behind its missions.

During significant events, such as the launch of the Cóndor-7 mission in September 2031, MASA coordinates global press coverage, ensuring that the achievements of Kuntur Station and its crew are celebrated worldwide. By fostering a culture of openness, MASA strengthens public trust and reinforces its commitment to advancing space exploration for the benefit of all humanity.
